Visual Interpretations & Data Stories

Market Analysis

How Book Prices are Distributed

This histogram with a Kernel Density Estimate (KDE) line shows how book prices are spread across the bookstore inventory. The vertical lines represent the average price (red dashed line) and median price (orange solid line).

Decision Insight: The distribution is relatively uniform between £10 and £60, with no severe outliers. This suggests a flat pricing model where items are evenly distributed across budget brackets, supporting retail strategies aiming for wide audience accessibility.
Customer Sentiment

Distribution of Consumer Ratings

This bar plot lists the frequency of books per star-rating category from 1 to 5. It reveals the volume of customer reviews across the platform.

Decision Insight: Ratings are highly balanced, with each bracket containing 18% to 22% of total inventory. No single rating dominates, indicating unbiased consumer feedback or a broad range of content quality across products.
Product Portfolio

Average Book Prices by Category

This horizontal bar chart highlights the top 10 most expensive genres by average retail price.

Decision Insight: Categories like 'Suspense' and 'Novels' average over £54 per book, making them premium product lines. Marketing budgets and premium promotional banners should target these high-margin categories to maximize revenue per sale.
Statistical Validation

Book Prices Across Rating Levels

This box-and-whisker plot compares price ranges across different ratings. The boxes show the IQR (Interquartile Range) and the horizontal line shows the median price.

Decision Insight: The box plots are remarkably aligned and overlap extensively. This visually validates our statistical hypothesis test, confirming that rating does not drive pricing. Premium products are priced high regardless of whether they have a 1-star or 5-star rating.
